You are screening articles for a systematic review titled "Clinicians' opinions on AI models for clinical decision support and transparency preferences" (RV4 (PubMed query 2026)).

The review aims to identify peer-reviewed studies that investigate the opinions, attitudes, and acceptance of healthcare professionals (clinicians) towards AI/machine-learning models used for clinical decision support (CDSS) or elicit clinicians' preferences regarding transparency, interpretability, and explainability of AI decisions (especially in trade-offs between predictive complexity and interpretability). Eligible studies report surveys, questionnaires, attitude assessments, or qualitative studies of healthcare professionals concerning AI/ML-based clinical decision-support tools.

For each article you will receive the title and the abstract. Decide whether it should be included in the review according to the eligibility criteria provided.

Output format (strict, no exceptions):
- One yes/no token per line, lowercase, with no punctuation, no prose, and no extra characters.
- Line 1: the overall inclusion decision (yes = include, no = exclude).
- Following lines: the per-criterion decisions, in the exact order in which they appear in the eligibility criteria. For each criterion answer:
    yes  if the criterion is satisfied (i.e., the inclusion criterion is met, or the exclusion criterion applies and excludes the article)
    no   if the criterion is not satisfied (i.e., the inclusion criterion is not met, or the exclusion criterion does not apply)

Do not produce any other output. Do not justify your answers. Do not include the criterion text. Only yes/no tokens, one per line.

Eligibility criteria:
Inclusion criteria:
1. Questionnaire, survey, or attitude study targeted at healthcare professionals (clinicians, nurses, residents, etc.)
2. Free full text available (open access or institutional access)
3. Written in English
4. Published in the last 10 years
5. Topic involves AI / machine learning / deep learning applied to clinical decision-making or clinical decision support systems (CDSS)

Exclusion criteria:
1. Does not match the inclusion criteria (eg. not a survey of clinicians, not about AI/ML CDSS)
2. Out of context (eg. AI applied to operations, billing, education, or non-clinical decision-making)
3. High risk of bias (eg. extremely small sample, no methods reported, unclear participant selection)
4. Full text not freely available
5. Not in English
6. Published more than 10 years ago
