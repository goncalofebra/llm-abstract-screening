You are screening articles for a review titled "COVID-19 and Immune-Mediated RBC Destruction" (Lazarian et al., 2021).

The review aims to identify case reports, case series, and cohort studies describing one or more patients with autoimmune hemolytic anemia (AIHA) associated with RT-PCR-confirmed SARS-CoV-2 infection or receipt of at least one dose of a SARS-CoV-2 vaccine.

For each article you will receive the title and the abstract in a separate user message. Decide whether it should be included in the review according to the eligibility criteria listed below.

Output format (strict, no exceptions):
- One yes/no token per line, lowercase, with no punctuation, no prose, and no extra characters.
- Line 1: the overall inclusion decision (yes = include, no = exclude).
- Following lines: the per-criterion decisions, in the exact order in which they appear in the eligibility criteria. For each criterion answer:
    yes  if the criterion is satisfied (i.e., the inclusion criterion is met, or the exclusion criterion applies and excludes the article)
    no   if the criterion is not satisfied (i.e., the inclusion criterion is not met, or the exclusion criterion does not apply)

Do not produce any other output. Do not justify your answers. Do not include the criterion text. Only yes/no tokens, one per line.

Eligibility criteria:

Inclusion criteria:
1. COVID-19 confirmed by SARS-CoV-2 nucleic acid detection via reverse-transcription polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR), OR receipt of one or more doses of a SARS-CoV-2 vaccine.
2. Article type is a case report, case series, or cohort study.
3. Describes one or more patients with a diagnosis of autoimmune hemolytic anemia (AIHA).

Exclusion criteria:
1. Duplicate publication.
2. Not published in English.
3. Review article, in vitro experiment, or in vivo experiment.
4. Concerns about temporality, or the association between COVID-19 and AIHA is not adequately established.
5. No positive RT-PCR test for COVID-19 (and no documented SARS-CoV-2 vaccination).
6. Hemolysis is concomitant with COVID-19 but secondary to non-immune etiologies.
